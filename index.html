Around the world, the country of Venezuela was famous for its large oil reserves, beautiful women, stunning landscapes, agriculture and distinct natural regions. Today, Venezuela is known for its shortage on basic goods such as food, medicine, water, and electricity. It is also notorious for its hyperinflation, corrupt government, and highest crime rates in the world. According to the Freedom House, an organizations that reports on levels of democracy around the world.Venezuela has been rated as a not free country with one of the worst rates for freedom, political rights and civil liberties.

Venezuela crisis started when the global prices for oil felt from $111 per barrel in 2014 to $27 per barrel in 2016. The fall of the oil cause a great economic chaos in Venezuela because 95 % of the export is oil. These led to a critical economic and political crisis that transform into extreme food and medicine shortages, rising crimes rate, unemployment, massive protests, and hyperflexion rates in the economy. Venezuela’s economic disaster are the compilation of years of economic mismanagement and the current fall of the barrel of oil in the market. The International Energy Statistics has measure the decrese of petroleum and other liquids of production in Venezuela.

Venezuela economic crisis has led to a sky-high inflation and deficiencies of food, medicines, and basic necessities like toilet paper, soap, toothpaste, and shampoo. Professionals opinions states that the government strict price controls, which were meant to keep basic goods affordable, are slightly to blame. In the country, several manufactures have decided to reduce production because of the limits there is to charge for their products. Imports felt, making foreign-made goods too expensive to purchase. Various buyers were faced with the choice of waiting in line for basic goods or paying overpriced goods that were being re-sell by bachaqueros or black-market traffickers.

Another economic issue is the currency control. It was first introduced by Chavez in 2003 to control U.S currency. The government implement it by selling U.S dollars at different rates, they created a black market and enhanced opportunities for corruption. For instance, a business that was authorized to but dollars at preferential rates to purchase basic goods like food and medicine could instead sell those dollars for a higher rate to a. third party. December 2017, the official exchange rate was 10 bolivares to the dollar while in the black-market rate was more than 9000 bolivares to the dollar.

The government of Venezuela executed the law of Fair Prices to keep the cost of products inexpensive. This law cause that many companies lost their revenues which also cause them to stop manufacturing their products which made the shortage worse. As a result, Venezuelan have turned to expensive imports or to buy in the black market. For many citizen, basic items like eggs and rice have become overpriced and they can not afford it. Venezuela minimum wage in U.S dollars is equivalent to eight dollars. Making Venezuela citizen with minimum wage poor that they can only buy six percent of the basic goods. In 2016, the Venezuelan Pharmaceutical Federation estimated that 85 % of basic medicine where unavailable or hard to obtain. Hospitals have absence of supplies such as antibiotics, morphine, pain killers, gauze, soap among others. Diseases previously eliminated from the country have reemerged. Such as, cholera, diphtheria, malaria, Hepatitis A, Tuberculosis, Yellow Fever, typhoid among others. In 2016 infant mortality increased by 30 % and maternal mortality 65 % over two years prior, according to governmental figures. Other study, found that 30 percent of children were malnourished.
